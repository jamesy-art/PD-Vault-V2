## Overview

Marce López is a Colombian illustrator and surface pattern designer based in Madrid, Spain. Her work spans clothing, packaging, murals, stationery, textiles, magazines, books, and digital media. She operates as a freelance practitioner serving Spanish and international brands. Selected Instagram images confirm a botanical, illustrative pattern practice with recurring floral and organic motifs and a warm, colourful palette.

## Style and Aesthetic

Work combines botanical imagery, symbolic motifs, and a colourful, delicate execution. Florals are central — selected pattern images suggest layered botanical compositions with an expressive, hand-crafted quality. The overall register is personal and painterly rather than minimal or geometric. Instagram bio describes the practice as "botany, colour & poetic narratives."

## Techniques and Tools

Works across illustration, surface pattern design, and textile design. Ceramics is referenced as an additional creative discipline. No specific digital software stated; the visual character of selected patterns suggests an illustrative, hand-originated workflow.

## Markets and Clients

Active across fashion, packaging, stationery, textiles, and editorial. Documented clients include La Casita de Wendy (fashion, FW25 "Una habitación propia" collection — pattern design inspired by antique tablecloths), BackCouture, [[Noc Noc Estudio]] (FW21/22 Spirit collection), Ecke, Minueto, Shuz Shuz, Mueve tu Lengua, and Acciona Madrid. Collaborations include Izaskun Chinchilla Arquitecta (CasaDecor 2017) and Apple Tree Communication (mural for Hospital de la Princesa, Madrid).

Work is described as weaving together the botanical, the symbolic, and the poetic.
