  Tatjana Tscherepanov is a textile and mixed media artist based in south

  media art. She finds inspiration in nature, on hikes in the mountains, in the
  forest or on a distant beach. She hopes for the viewer to find curiosity and
  wonder in her art. What the Layers Hold is a body of work that explores
  memory, impermanence, and transformation through layered mixed-media
  compositions. Geometric structures meet organic gestures, creating pieces that
  feel both intentional and intuitive, weathered and alive. Each work invites
  slow looking and quiet discovery - an exploration of what time erases, what it
  inscribes deeper, and the hidden beauty found beneath the surface. What
  Remains Unsaid is a meditation on the quiet weight of memory - the thoughts,
  feelings, and stories we carry but never fully voice. Soft layers of texture
  and color blur the line between what is revealed and what stays hidden,
  inviting the viewer into a space of reflection and quiet curiosity. This piece
  holds a subtle tension: strength and fragility, presence and absence,
  structure and erosion. It’s for the collector who finds beauty in imperfection

  This series of What the Layers Hold is a meditation on memory, impermanence,
  and the quiet transformations of time. Through layered printing, painting and
  collage, these mixed-media works explore what is visible on the surface - and
  what quietly remains beneath. Geometric grids and organic marks intertwine,
  reflecting the tension between structure and erosion, control and surrender.
  Circles echo cycles of nature, breath, and renewal, while weathered textures
  speak to aging, transformation, and the fragile beauty of imperfection. Built
  slowly and intuitively, each piece carries the rhythm and patience of
  handcraft, inviting the viewer to slow down and discover subtle details that
  emerge with time. These works are not immediate answers but quiet invitations
  - to look closely, to feel deeply, and to consider what your own layers hold.
