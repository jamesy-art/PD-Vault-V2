website_meta_description: >-
  I am a graduate of the Northern School of Art with a BA (Hons) Textiles &
  Surface Design. I have a strong interest in digital drawing and printing as
  well as hand embroidery and embellishments. Not only that, but I hope with my
  time on this course I am able to expand into a creative career or that this
  course will aid me in getting a job I enjoy and wish to pursue.During my time
  on this course, I have had the wonderful opportunity to not only learn new
  skills but expand and develop my previous skills. One technique I had the
  opportunity to develop was my Photoshop skills and my digital pattern making
  skills. My tutor was able to teach me various ways of creating patterns (such
  as half drop, multi-directional and mirror repeats.) Alongside this I was also
  taught how to create industry style patterns and how to create designs that
  fit industry sizes and standards. When starting this course, I had already
  attended the UAL Extended Diploma Textiles & Surface Design at The Northern
  School of Arts Middlesbrough campus. On that course I was able to learn basic
  design skills and techniques which I was able to carry over to my degree level
  course, as mentioned previously.My current theme is 'Royal & Regality' which
  is a visual exploration of British Royalty and its ornate aesthetic. My final
  collection includes fabrics, wallpaper and cushions with designs all made for
  children’s interiors. This concept idea came after a trip to London during the
  holidays, where I was lucky enough to visit Buckingham Palace and explore some
  rooms inside and all the art and furniture within them. The idea of exploring
  royalty also came from my love of history and specifically royal history, as I
  have always had a love for learning history since I was a child. The reason
  behind choosing to explore British royalty is to look at the extravagance and
  the elegance that is has and the deep-rooted traditions within it. Part of my
  brief was to participate in group work on the lead up to my degree show. I was
  put in the first group whose key role was gather personal statements, show
  promotion and the degree show rota. I was tasked with gathering business
  stationary (business cards, CVs and invoices). From this I was able to learn
  how to work with others in a professional manner. I also had the role of
  sending follow-up emails to fellow students in preparation for the degree
  show. Overall, I am hoping my time on this course will have helped and
  prepared me for my future career. After university I am hoping to progress
  into a career in museums. Alongside my degree I have been a part of the Young
  Producers, who are a local youth group who work with young people and museums
  to bring a younger generation to museums. I am hoping with my time at both
  establishments and all the skills and experience I have learnt I can progress
  into a career in museums or a creative sector.
