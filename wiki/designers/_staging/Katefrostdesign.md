  blending design, art, and mindfulness. Learn how she combines creativity and
  self-care. I’m Kate Frost, a surface-pattern designer, artist, and
  storyteller. I now live in the Somerset countryside after growing up in South
  London. My creative path has always been shaped by rhythm, pattern, and a love
  of design that feels both expressive and intentional. As a young adult in
  London, I was deeply influenced by the energy of the underground music scene.
  Drum & bass, old school house, dubstep and heavy bass. I guess I’ve always
  loved pattern, whether through sound or visuals. That sense of movement and
  repetition naturally found its way into my exploration of Celtic knotwork,
  mandalas, geometric patterns, and other early artwork I loved to make. In my
  late teens and twenties I worked in the financial services, then after
  travelling through Thailand and living in Australia for a while, I returned to
  the UK, and my husband and I made Somerset our home. Its history, landscape,
  and sense of spaciousness felt grounding in a way I hadn’t experienced before.
  I feel like I’m living in a Tolkien novel! We’ve raised our two boys here, and
  caring for my eldest, who is autistic and learning disabled, has shaped so
  much of the person and artist I’ve become. His needs opened the door to yoga
  and meditation, first as personal practices and eventually as something I
  taught. Those practices helped me stay steady through the demands of caring,
  while also reminding me of the one thing I still missed deeply: making art. In
  2020, I committed to bringing physical art-making back into the centre of my
  life. A hand-drawn & painted mandala was accepted into the Black Swan Arts

  keep going. Soon after, I discovered the world of digital surface pattern
