website_about_clean: >-
  About the Artist I’m a New Jersey-based illustrator and Surface Print/Pattern
  Designer. I attended Moore College of Art and Design where I received a BFA in
  illustration. Since that time, I have created projects for a variety of
  clients including Hudson Valley Seed Co., the Smithsonian, the US Department
  of Agriculture, Urban Outfitters Home, and Anthropologie. In my creative
  journey as an illustrator and surface print designer, I find inspiration in
  the intricate beauty of the natural world. Nature, with its boundless
  diversity and delicate balance, serves as my ultimate muse. My artistic
  process involves meticulous observation, as I immerse myself in the details of
  each subject. While my primary focus is botanical illustration, my passion for
  the living world extends to encompass a fascination with birds and other
  animals. By capturing their grace and vitality through art, I aim to bridge
  the gap between human society and the wild, encouraging a deeper understanding
  and appreciation for these creatures' importance in our shared ecosystems. As
  a surface pattern designer, I harness the patterns and forms that nature
  provides, transforming them into designs for a variety of surfaces, from
  textiles to stationery. Through my patterns, I seek to instill a sense of
  wonder and connection with the natural world, inviting viewers to explore the
  delicate dance between repetition and variation, order and spontaneity. My
  creative philosophy is rooted in a deep respect for ecological balance and
  sustainable practices. I believe that by celebrating the beauty of plants and
  animals, we can also cultivate an awareness of their fragility and the
  importance of their preservation. With this in mind, my work often carries
  subtle messages of environmental stewardship. Please contact with questions,
  comments, or with inquiries about freelancing at
  mfillustration.studio@gmail.com or by filling out a contact form on the site.

  About the Artist

  I’m a New Jersey-based illustrator and Surface Print/Pattern Designer. I
  attended Moore College of Art and Design where I received a BFA in
  illustration. Since that time, I have created projects for a variety of
  clients including Hudson Valley Seed Co., the Smithsonian, the US Department
  of Agriculture, Urban Outfitters Home, and Anthropologie. 


  In my creative journey as an illustrator and surface print designer, I find
  inspiration in the intricate beauty of the natural world. Nature, with its
  boundless diversity and delicate balance, serves as my ultimate muse. My
  artistic process involves meticulous observation, as I immerse myself in the
  details of each subject.  


  While my primary focus is botanical illustration, my passion for the living
  world extends to encompass a fascination with birds and other animals. By
  capturing their grace and vitality through art, I aim to bridge the gap
  between human society and the wild, encouraging a deeper understanding and
  appreciation for these creatures' importance in our shared ecosystems.


  As a surface pattern designer, I harness the patterns and forms that nature
  provides, transforming them into designs for a variety of surfaces, from
  textiles to stationery. Through my patterns, I seek to instill a sense of
  wonder and connection with the natural world, inviting viewers to explore the
  delicate dance between repetition and variation, order and spontaneity.


  My creative philosophy is rooted in a deep respect for ecological balance and
  sustainable practices. I believe that by celebrating the beauty of plants and
  animals, we can also cultivate an awareness of their fragility and the
  importance of their preservation. With this in mind, my work often carries
  subtle messages of environmental stewardship.
