## Overview

Alice Marshall is a UK-based freelance print designer and retoucher. She currently works at [[Meri Meri]] as a photographer and retoucher while maintaining an independent print design practice. Her design portfolio demonstrates a conceptual, narrative approach to surface pattern — with collections rooted in personal heritage, regional landscape, and travel destinations. She also sells via Etsy.

## Style and Aesthetic

Marshall's print work is narrative and location-specific. Collections — "The Lost Gardens of Keepers Cottage" (a 100-year-old family property in Doncaster), "Yorkshire's Heritage," "Homage to the North York Moors," and "Kefalonian Kitchenware" — suggest an illustrative, place-informed aesthetic rather than trend-led commercial output. Selected Instagram images suggest textured surface patterns and decorative repeats informed by botanical and heritage subjects.

## Techniques and Tools

Skills listed include pattern design and colour theory. Academic project work documents patchworking, upcycling integration (repurposing garments), and garment design alongside print — indicating a broad textile foundation. Professional retouching and product photography skills from her role at Meri Meri provide strong production and presentation literacy.
