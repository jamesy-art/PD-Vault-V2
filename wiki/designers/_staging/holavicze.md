  My story ​ I grew up surrounded by colors and textures: lush wallpapers, plush
  rugs, and patterned fabrics brought every corner of my house to life. My
  father and grandfather owned a home decor shop with exquisite taste, and it
  was there that my fascination with the visual and textile world was born. From
  a young age, I dreamed that one day my drawings could become part of that
  universe. In the margins of my school notebooks, I would already imagine
  wallpapers with borders and coordinating prints, drawn with colorful markers.
  I studied Interior Design, but over time painting took up more and more space
  in my life. An invitation to design fabrics in Brazil opened the doors to the
  world of print design, and I haven't stopped since. Around that same time, I
  started painting murals and fell in love with walls and their challenges: each
  one with its own texture, scale, and story. Since then, I have continued to
  explore ways to transform spaces into visual experiences that invite you to
  stop, observe, and reconnect with the beauty of nature. For me, drawing,
  painting, or printing is a way of paying attention and sharing that
  perspective. My inspiration For me, inspiration is everywhere. I like to go
  for walks without a destination, taking photos and recording the shapes,
  patterns, and colors that appear along the way. My work is very much like
  that: observing carefully and returning again and again to the sources, where
  everything is. I live in a small town by the sea, and the surroundings, the
  natural rhythm of things, and the everyday beauty move me every day. I'm
  fascinated by the way plants grow, how they intertwine, their rhythm, and the
  patterns they create. I'm also inspired by architectural details, especially
  handcrafted ornamentation, which conveys history and dedication. Painting,
  ceramics, design, and illustration are the crafts that sustain me and
  intertwine in my work. Sometimes I screen print on fabrics, other times I
  paint them by hand, always searching for new ways to experiment. I deeply
  enjoy the direct contact with materials and every stage of the creative
  process. I work from experience: observing, testing, making mistakes, and
  starting again. That's where I find the ideas that drive me to keep creating.
  My job I am a visual artist and illustrator, specializing in print design and
  mural painting. I create works that transform spaces and objects through
  color, shapes, and textures. I design digital prints for brands, develop
  illustrations, and paint murals adapted to different contexts, both indoors
  and outdoors, for private or commercial projects. I enjoy challenges and new
  projects. I especially enjoy working on ideas as part of a team, collaborating
  with others, and developing customized proposals with each client. Every
  project is an opportunity to visually explore an idea and find the graphic
  language that best expresses it. I worked with brands such as KENZO PARFUMS,
  ARREDO; CERAMICA SAN LORENZO, CITIBANK, HINDS, DESIDERATA, MAZALOSA,
  FARMACITY, BAFWEEK and SIMPLICITY among others.

  My story

  ​


  I grew up surrounded by colors and textures: lush wallpapers, plush rugs, and
  patterned fabrics brought every corner of my house to life. My father and
  grandfather owned a home decor shop with exquisite taste, and it was there
  that my fascination with the visual and textile world was born. From a young
  age, I dreamed that one day my drawings could become part of that universe. In
  the margins of my school notebooks, I would already imagine wallpapers with
  borders and coordinating prints, drawn with colorful markers.


  I studied Interior Design, but over time painting took up more and more space
  in my life. An invitation to design fabrics in Brazil opened the doors to the
  world of print design, and I haven't stopped since. Around that same time, I
  started painting murals and fell in love with walls and their challenges: each
  one with its own texture, scale, and story.


  Since then, I have continued to explore ways to transform spaces into visual
  experiences that invite you to stop, observe, and reconnect with the beauty of
  nature. For me, drawing, painting, or printing is a way of paying attention
  and sharing that perspective.


  My inspiration

  For me, inspiration is everywhere. I like to go for walks without a
  destination, taking photos and recording the shapes, patterns, and colors that
  appear along the way. My work is very much like that: observing carefully and
  returning again and again to the sources, where everything is. I live in a
  small town by the sea, and the surroundings, the natural rhythm of things, and
  the everyday beauty move me every day.


  I'm fascinated by the way plants grow, how they intertwine, their rhythm, and
  the patterns they create. I'm also inspired by architectural details,
  especially handcrafted ornamentation, which conveys history and dedication.


  Painting, ceramics, design, and illustration are the crafts that sustain me
  and intertwine in my work. Sometimes I screen print on fabrics, other times I
  paint them by hand, always searching for new ways to experiment. I deeply
  enjoy the direct contact with materials and every stage of the creative
  process.


  I work from experience: observing, testing, making mistakes, and starting
  again. That's where I find the ideas that drive me to keep creating.


  My job
