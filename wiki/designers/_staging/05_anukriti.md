## Overview

Anukriti Didwania is an India-based textile designer, print-maker, and illustrator. She holds a degree from the National Institute of Fashion Technology (NIFT), Himachal Pradesh, and works freelance across print design for fashion, interiors, and textiles. Her practice is rooted in a deep engagement with florals, natural dyes, and Indian textile traditions. Selected Instagram images suggest a hand-crafted, illustrative approach to botanical and floral print work, with some character-based and mixed-motif compositions.

## Style and Aesthetic

Floral-led with a hand-made sensibility. Selected pattern images suggest rich botanical compositions — layered florals, delicate organic motifs — rendered with warmth and intricacy. Self-initiated project titles in the website ("Peri Head," "Dancing Jewels," "Happy Bloom," "Local Bloom") indicate a personal visual vocabulary alongside commercial work. The practice bridges decorative illustration and textile print design.

## Techniques and Tools

Trained in textile design at NIFT (Himachal Pradesh). Works with mood boards and colour boards at every design stage. Explores natural dyeing as both a creative and sustainability practice — referenced in Instagram bio ("Constantly touring with Natural Dye"). Applies prints across fashion, interior, and textile markets.

## Markets and Clients

Fashion and textiles are the primary markets. Documented clients include SUI (multiple collections: Summer 2022 "Ocean Tide," Winter 2021 "Bloom," Winter 2020 "Wildflower"), Nainsi Agarwal (kids' print, watercolour floral), Margarit (toile print), Essgee (Wedding in Italy print), Slow Fabric (Jamdani Label), Meesa, Auruhfy, and Madrush. The range of clients spans womenswear, kidswear, and heritage textile applications.

## Career Path

Moved to Himachal Pradesh in 2015 to pursue textile design studies at NIFT. Following graduation, established a freelance practice across print-making, illustration, and textile design. Career has engaged both Indian fashion brands and smaller independent labels. Personal practice includes exploration of natural dyes and self-initiated illustrative projects.

## Pattern Focus

Selected patterns suggest botanical floral all-overs and layered organic compositions, alongside illustrative work with figurative elements (Peri Head series, Dancing Jewels). Floral prints are the consistent commercial focus — documented across womenswear, kidswear, and heritage-style applications (Jamdani label, toile print for Margarit). The natural dye interest introduces an additional material dimension to the practice.
