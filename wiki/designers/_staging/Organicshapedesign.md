  All About Tabitha &

  Organic Shape Design...

  Tabitha was raised to be mindful of our natural environment, under the wing of
  her all-knowing Grandmother. Forever curious and always seeking new knowledge
  in regards to the science of nature, she enjoys learning about the anatomy of
  flora & fauna through illustration. Her history of being connected to nature
  is reflected in her portfolio of work, but also in how she lives. Gardening
  and being self-sufficient is something she enjoys greatly, and this ultimately
  feeds into the inspiration for her designs and illustrations. Her main career
  objective in relation to design is to design for the greater good, with her
  work providing spotlight for our natural environment, educating others on its
  importance and being sustainable in her practice. 

   

  From Tabitha's history, Organic Shape Design is committed to representing the
  beauty of our natural environment through design and artistry. Tabitha's
  design journey is focused on creating intricate and unique illustrations for
  surface applications that showcase the wonders of nature. Tabitha's designs
  are carefully crafted to inspire others to appreciate and protect our planet.


  Tabitha possesses a wealth of qualities that are highly relevant to the
  digital drawing and CAD techniques used in the design industry. Her portfolio
  showcases her unique style and creativity, which is inspired by nature and
  features organic shapes and patterns that are both aesthetically pleasing and
  functional. She holds a Bachelor's Degree in Textile Design, after graduating
  from Norwich University of the Arts in July of 2024. Throughout her years at
  university, she dedicated each project to celebrating various aspects of
  nature. You can view the portfolio of work created in her time at university
  on the Portfolio page. 


  ​​


  ​Tabitha has a keen interest in working with individuals, charities and
  organisations that share a similar ethos and approach to preserving,
  protecting and celebrating our natural environment. Despite holding a
  Bachelor's in Textile Design, Tabitha holds the qualities of an illustrator,
  digital artworker and artist, therefore allowing for a skillset crossover in
  terms of what projects she can successfully work on. She is an independent
  designer, illustrator and maker, providing services to those who have an
  interest in her design-mind, but also providing slow-made textile pieces that
  represent her ethos of celebrating nature.
