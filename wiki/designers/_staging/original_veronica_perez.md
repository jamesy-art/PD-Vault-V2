  for those who value art that connects with their roots.” Vision “To be

  We are Original Verónica Pérez , an art and craft workshop run by the artist
  Verónica Pérez. From our creative foundation, we combine traditional
  techniques—papier-mâché, painted wood, illustration—with a fresh artistic
  vision.

  Each piece is unique: handcrafted with attention to detail and inspired by the
  festivities, colors, and energies of Mexico. We are driven by a passion for
  creating art that not only decorates but also evokes identity, emotion, and
  presence.

  More than a workshop, we are an art community that values ​​authenticity,
  cultural connection, and free expression. Thank you for joining us on this
  journey of creation, tradition, and originality.



  Mission

  “At Original Verónica Pérez, we create unique handcrafted works that celebrate
  the richness of Mexican tradition through techniques such as papier-mâché,
  painted wood, and illustration. Our commitment is to bring color,
  authenticity, and joy to every space, delivering lovingly handcrafted pieces
  for those who value art that connects with their roots.”


  Vision

  “To be recognized as a benchmark of contemporary craftsmanship that fuses
  Mexican tradition and individual creativity, inspiring collectors and art
  lovers in Mexico and around the world. We aspire to expand our reach through
  collaborations, workshops, and online presence, so that each creation by
  Verónica Pérez transcends and flourishes in new homes.”


  Achievements and Exhibitions


  Museums and Galleries

  My work has been presented and sold at renowned institutions such as the 
  Frida Kahlo Museum,  the  Museum of Anthropology and History  , and the 
  Estanquillo Museum. 


  International Recognition

  Some of my work is now part of the cultural heritage collection at the Mexican
  Institute in Paris, France , and has been acquired by the Mexican Institute in
  Atlanta, GA, for cultural purposes. They have also sold my work to the Museum
  of Popular Cultures in Mexico City .  




  Fact

  Special Recognition

  One of the proudest moments of my career was receiving a letter from Victoire
  de Castellane (creative director of the Jewelry Department at Maison Dior),
  who commented on my work: " Bravo for these extremely original creations that
  have found their place on my desk among my other sources of inspiration! "
