  Animals are simply wonderful, and luckily there are so many of them, so
  Kathrin from Käselotti certainly won't run out of ideas. She transforms the
  inspiration she finds everywhere into pictures and figures that bring a smile

  off with käselotti in 2007. Initially, the product range consisted of cell
  phone and tobacco pouches, which Kathrin sewed and sold on DaWanda and at
  design markets. Eventually, she discovered the possibility of having fabrics
  printed with her own designs, and one thing led to another, resulting in what
  käselotti is today.

  Animals are simply wonderful, and luckily there are so many of them, so
  Kathrin from Käselotti certainly won't run out of ideas. She transforms the
  inspiration she finds everywhere into pictures and figures that bring a smile
  to the faces of young and old alike.


  Being kind makes life so much easier and more relaxed. That's why Kathrin not
  only tries to be friendly to everyone around her, but for her work and
  illustrations, this also means portraying all animals in a friendly and
  positive light. Whether mosquito, jellyfish, or polar bear, every animal is
  equally valuable. And so that we can still experience this diversity of life
  for many decades to come, she pays as much attention as possible to
  sustainability and environmental compatibility in the raw materials she uses.
  Taking responsibility for nature and the environment is a particular concern
  for Käselotti.


  How it all began…

  Kathrin has actually been sewing, painting, and crafting since childhood, but
  things really took off with käselotti in 2007. Initially, the product range
  consisted of cell phone and tobacco pouches, which Kathrin sewed and sold on
  DaWanda and at design markets. Eventually, she discovered the possibility of
  having fabrics printed with her own designs, and one thing led to another,
  resulting in what käselotti is today.
