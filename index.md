## Types

- [[Surface Pattern Design]] — The discipline itself: definition, career models, tools, markets (stub)
- [[Fabric Pattern Classification]] — Two-tier taxonomy: Geometric vs. Organic, with Realistic/Stylized subdivisions
- [[Pattern Design Terminology]] — Core SPD vocabulary: layouts, repeats, elements, symmetry, classification axes
- [[Pattern Layout Types]] — Structural repeat rules (square, half-drop, brick, tossed, etc.)
- [[Wallpaper Groups]] — Mathematical classification of all 17 two-dimensional repeat pattern symmetry types; 7 frieze groups; Escher connection
- [[Historical Eras in Pattern Design]] — Arts and Crafts, Art Nouveau, Art Deco, Mid-Century Modern
- [[Production Techniques]] — Weaving, screen printing, digital printing, block printing, batik, ikat, embroidery, knitting
- [[wiki/types/Acheik]] — Burmese chain-weft silk weaving; distinctive ripple/wave pattern; traditional dress of Myanmar
- [[wiki/types/Adinkra Symbols]] — Akan/Ghanaian symbolic textile tradition; stamped cloth with semiotic depth; cultural appropriation context
- [[Animal Prints]] — Patterns mimicking animal skin/fur/scales; Organic/Realistic category; leopard/zebra/giraffe variants
- [[Arabesque]] — Intertwined curvilinear floral/geometric ornament; Islamic art origin; infinitely extendable
- [[Batik]] — Javanese wax-resist dyeing; crackle lines; UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage
- [[Ben Day Dots]] — Mechanical printing technique using uniform equal-sized dots; 1879; comic-book art; Roy Lichtenstein Pop Art connection
- [[wiki/types/Celtic Maze]] — Continuous-path knotwork/labyrinth pattern; Insular art; illuminated manuscripts
- [[Camouflage]] — Military disruptive pattern families (woodland, digital, splinter, tigerstripe); fashion adoption from hip-hop to luxury
- [[Checkered and Plaid Patterns]] — 15+ named variants including tartan, houndstooth, gingham, argyle; 3,000 years of woven check history
- [[Chevron and Herringbone]] — Zigzag pattern family; woven and printed; classic and contemporary
- [[Damask]] — Jacquard-woven reversible ornamental fabric; satin/sateen contrast; formal floral compositions
- [[Ditzy]] — Allover small-scale scattered buds/marks; eccentrically playful character
- [[Dotted Swiss]] — Lightweight sheer cotton fabric with small raised or woven dots; Swiss embroidery tradition
- [[Ethnic and Cultural Patterns]] — Patterns defined by geographic/cultural origin: African, Islamic, South Asian, European, Indigenous
- [[Floral and Botanical Patterns]] — Flower and plant motif patterns; from realistic to highly stylized
- [[wiki/types/Gingham]] — Plain-woven duotone check in equal-width stripes; Vichy check variant; classic fashion and home fabric
- [[Greek Key Pattern]] — Continuous right-angle interlocking border motif; antiquity to present; 23,000 BCE origin
- [[wiki/types/Guilloché]] — Engine-turned mathematical engraving of intricate interlaced curved lines; luxury goods and security printing
- [[Gul]] — Central Asian octagonal tribal medallion in Turkmen/Persian pile carpet tradition
- [[wiki/types/Halftone]] — Variable-sized dot reprographic technique simulating continuous tone; CMYK printing; retro/Pop Art aesthetic
- [[wiki/types/Harlequin Print]] — Allover diamond (45°-rotated check) pattern in alternating colours; commedia dell'arte origin
- [[wiki/types/Hedebo Embroidery]] — Danish regional whitework embroidery; cutwork and needlepoint lace; Zealand tradition
- [[Herati]] — Persian carpet motif: stylised rosette in a diamond with serrated fish/leaf forms
- [[wiki/types/Houndstooth]] — Duotone tessellation from Bronze Age Austria; woven 2:2 twill; Dior/McQueen association
- [[wiki/types/Ikat]] — Yarn-dyed-before-weaving; blurred feathered edges; Indonesian/Central Asian/Japanese traditions
- [[Indienne]] — 17th–18th C European imitation of Indian block-print textiles; Provence fabric tradition
- [[wiki/types/Ise Katagami]] — Japanese paper stencil craft for dyeing textiles; resist-dyeing ancestor of screen printing; UNESCO heritage
- [[wiki/types/Islamic Geometric Patterns]] — Star-and-polygon tradition from 9th-century Islamic art; mathematically precise; all 17 wallpaper groups
- [[Kilim]] — Persian flat-weave rug geometric patterns; symbolic motif vocabulary; angular, diagonal, bold colour
- [[Liberty Style]] — Small-scale allover organic floral print; Art Nouveau; associated with Liberty & Co
- [[Lishui]] — Multicoloured diagonal sea-wave border on Qing dynasty Chinese imperial court robes; cosmological symbolism
- [[Millefleurs]] — Thousand-flowers medieval tapestry background; naturalistically depicted flowers on dark ground
- [[Moire]] — Watered-silk optical ripple effect; interference patterns; security printing applications
- [[Moroccan Lattice]] — Trellis variant with two pointed + two rounded sides; North African architectural origin
- [[Ogee]] — S-curve double-curve cell; architectural origin 12th C; versatile repeat framework
- [[wiki/types/Okir]] — Philippine Maranao/Moro plant-based decorative folk motif; male/female curvilinear/rectilinear traditions
- [[Ombre]] — Gradual tonal or colour gradient; dark-to-light or colour-to-colour transition
- [[Optical Art]] — Abstract pattern creating visual illusions of movement; 1960s fine art movement
- [[wiki/types/Overlapping Circles Grid]] — Hexagonal grid of overlapping circles; Flower of Life; oldest documented ornament; Assyrian origin c. 700 BCE
- [[Paisley]] — Persian boteh motif; Kashmir shawl history; named for Scottish town; 1960s psychedelic revival
- [[Parzenica]] — Polish Goral highlander heart-shaped embroidery motif; Podhale region; folk costume
- [[Polka Dot]] — Filled circles equally spaced in a set layout; one of SPD's most universal patterns
- [[Quatrefoil]] — Four-rounded-side trellis; Gothic and heraldic origins
- [[wiki/types/Quincunx]] — Five-point arrangement (four corners + centre) underlying half-drop and diagonal repeat grids; Cosmatesque mosaic; Angkor Wat
- [[Scale and Clamshell]] — Overlapping arcs mimicking fish scales; universal across cultures
- [[Stripe Patterns]] — Linear pattern family; 15+ named variants by width, colour, and regularity
- [[Sunbonnet Sue]] — American quilting character motif; faceless girl in sunbonnet; feminist reinterpretation history
- [[Sussi]] — Pakistani/Indian handloom textile; striped and checked silk-cotton blend; Sindh/Punjab tradition
- [[Talim]] — Kashmiri symbolic weaving notation system; pre-digital textile programming for kani shawls and carpets; GI-protected
- [[wiki/types/Tartan]] — Scottish woven check identified by sett; clan/family/regimental associations; Vestiarium Scoticum controversy
- [[Toile de Jouy]] — Scenic narrative print; single colour on white/light ground; 18th C French origin
- [[wiki/types/Traditional Patterns of Korea]] — Korean symbolic pattern vocabulary across four historical periods; Ten Symbols of Longevity
- [[Trailing Patterns]] — Branch/vine trailing through design with flowers, leaves, birds; directional
- [[Trellis and Lattice]] — Interlacing grid framework; most fundamental repeat structure in decorative design
- [[wiki/types/Truchet Tile]] — Asymmetric square tiles generating complex emergent patterns; mathematical tiling system; generative art
- [[wiki/types/Whorl]] — Spiral or concentric circular form; botanical, mollusc, fingerprint, and hair whorls; foundational motif in scroll and spiral patterns
- [[Zellij]] — Moroccan geometric mosaic tilework; intricate enamelled terracotta tile patterns
